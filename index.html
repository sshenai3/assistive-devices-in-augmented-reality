About ADLs

Assistive device is a specially designed device to assist people who have difficulty to perform activities of daily living. It can be applied in different situations, including dressing, feeding, toileting, bathing, grooming, household activities) and mobility (lifting and transfer). Commonly used devices can be classified into these categories.

What is ADR?

ADR (assistive device resource) is a web directory/ resource focused towards Assisitive Devices for Activities of Daily Living (ADLs). Here you will find a collection of assistive devices refined with all categories and also viewable through augmented Reality helping you visualize these products.

Product Search Categories

Meal Preparation

Assisitve devices related to planning, preparing, and serving meals and cleaning up food and tools (e.g., utensils, pots, plates) after meals. These are categorized under cooking, eating, drinking, and cleaning.

Image Credits

Personal Hygiene

Assisitve devices are categorized for actvities related to maintaining personal hygiene such as Bathing/ Showering (using supplies, soaping, rinsing, and drying body parts, etc), Grooming (washing, drying, combing, styling, brushing, and trimming hair; caring for nails, etc), Oral Care (brushing and flossing teeth, removing, cleaning, and reinserting dental orthotics and prosthetics, etc) and Toiletting (using toileting supplies, managing clothing, cleaning body, etc).

Image Credits

Dressing and Undressing

Assistive devices related to selecting clothing and accessories; obtaining clothing from storage area; dressing and undressing in a sequential fashion; fastening and adjusting clothing and shoes; applying and removing personal devices, prosthetic devices, or splints.

Image Credits

Functional Mobility/ Positioning

Assisitve devices related to added accessories (for wheelchair, cane, etc), in-home mobility, community mobility, transfers. Moving from one position or place to another (during performance of everyday activities), such as in-bed mobility, wheelchair mobility, and transfers (e.g., wheelchair, bed, car, shower, tub, toilet, chair, floor).
